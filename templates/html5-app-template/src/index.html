OUR MISSION. HOPE.

Hope is a little word with such a big meaning.

Hope is at the foundation of all we do, especially in research. We believe in the promise of a brighter future, and we conduct research to help make that belief become reality.

Part of this commitment involves conducting medical research with the goal of helping patients live longer, healthier lives. Through discovery science and clinical studies, researchers can better understand how to diagnose, treat, prevent, and cure diseases or conditions.

Mayo Clinic's innovative research is accelerating discoveries and cures for patients.

ADVANCING PATIENT CARE

What our research looks like and how it works

Medical research is more than just collecting and studying data— it’s about finding real cures that help patients.

Utilizing a wealth of data, including genetics, biobanks, digital images, and clinical records, we drive innovation to deliver new knowledge and insights. With our unique strengths and resources, we are speeding up discoveries and creating better ways to diagnose, treat, prevent, and cure disease.

To explain some of the process, here are two important steps in bringing new treatments to patients:

DISCOVERY
SCIENCE

Uncovering how diseases work
at the most fundamental level.

This is where research often begins. Scientists explore the biological processes of diseases at a cellular and molecular level, testing ideas in laboratory settings. Their work helps identify potential treatments and medical innovations before they reach patient trials.

CLINICAL
TRIALS

Testing and refining treatments
to bring them to patients.

Once a potential treatment or approach shows promise in discovery science, it moves into clinical trials. Here, physicians and researchers work with patients to test new treatments, drugs, or medical devices—ensuring they are safe and effective before they become standard care.

MULTIDISCIPLINARY TEAMS

At Mayo Clinic, research isn’t done in silos. Our unique culture of collaboration and teamwork, and our extensive facilities and resources, make it possible for researchers to unravel and solve complex research questions.

This close integration makes it possible to quickly bring new technologies, therapies, and cures to patients and share this knowledge with the next generation of doctors and scientists.

GET INVOLVED

Research needs people like you.

There are many ways to get involved
in or support research.

1

Participate in a research study

Some study participation is very simple, like completing a survey or giving a blood sample. Other study activities are more complicated and may involve study visits, medications, or procedures. Each study is unique, and you can always choose whether you would like to participate.

2

Allow your medical records to be used in research

Some studies observe patients’ health through the medical record over a period of time. This allows researchers to see how diseases progress and learn from larger groups of people.

3

Join a research volunteer registry

Volunteer registries allow you to proactively sign-up to be contacted about potential research opportunities.
